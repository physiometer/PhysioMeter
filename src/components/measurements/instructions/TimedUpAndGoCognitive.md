Set Up:

- Position 17 inch high armchair against a wall, column, or heavy piece of furniture for stability.
- Place a cone or marker 3 meters from the front edge of the chair.
- Participant seated in chair with both feet touching the floor, place a pillow if necessary to ensure feet touch the floor.
- Participant to use regular footwear and walking aid.

Script:

- You'll start by sitting here with your back touching the back of the chair, your feet on the floor, and your arms resting on the armrests. [If your back can't reach the back of the chair with your feet on the ground, I'll put a pillow behind you.]
- I'll give you a number, and you will need to keep subtracting 3 from that number while you move, like this 79, 76, 73, 70, 67, 64, 61, 58...
- When I say go, stand up, walk at your usual pace to the target, turn around, walk back to the chair, and sit entirely down again, all the while subtracting your 3s!
- Do you have any questions? (Tester clarifies the task procedure as necessary)
- The number I'd like you to start subtracting from is 51.
- Ready, Set, Go!

Tester Instructions:

- Stand next to the participant to guard in case of instability during testing.
- After giving instructions (and demonstration), begin timing as you say "Go."
- Walk next to the participant during testing in case of instability.
- Stop timing as soon as the participant's buttocks are on the seat.
- Count the number of errors in the subtraction sequence.
